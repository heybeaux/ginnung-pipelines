# A Traveler's Tattoo

We all know someone who has a tattoo, or two, or in my case, 27. My younger sister has more ink than most Hells Angels and for a number of years I myself have considered getting "inked". Like most people though, I was a little nervous about getting my first tattoo. So nervous in fact that it took me nearly a decade to decide what to get and where to get it.

Back? Maybe, but I wouldn't be able to see it.

Leg? Nah, it'd make shorts look… Odd.

Bum? Hmmmm…. Mum will never see it. Bonus!

Side? Are you kidding? Do you know how painful that is?

Penis? … What? Why? Seriously? I'd rather use my testicles as a doorstop.

So after much deliberation I decided to not think about it at all and simply make a spur of the moment decision when I got to the parlour. Classic.

Now, those of you reading this are surely thinking "wait, I know how this plays out". You expect that that I'd walk in, make some comment about "not being sure" or "what looks good" and then walking out with a tattoo of a pink spotted dragon drinking tea with the Mad Hatter whilst riding a unicycle with the words "Hamsters 4eva" spelled incorrectly somewhere around my inner thigh region.

Some twisted part of me would like to say "you're right!" and then upload the pictures of my colourful new inner thigh (sans visible testicles of course). However, that's not the way this story goes. It turned out that I had, in fact, put quite a bit of thought into my tattoo. I wanted the words "de mortuis nil nisi bonum" across my chest. These words are a quote from the P. C. Wren novel, my namesake, "Beaux Geste".

Let's run through this quickly. I know what I want and where I want to get it. It seems like all I have to do now is take off my pants and get inked. I know I don't need to take off my pants to get a tattoo on my chest, but I just find it more relaxing.

Wait! Like the Star Wars saga, I've left a crucial plot point out of the start of my story. What I strategically forgot to mention is that I planned to get my first tattoo in Thailand.

Yes, Thailand.

I know that hygiene isn't their major focus there. Yes, I know an artist there doesn't need any discernible amount of talent to start a parlour. And sure, the Chang beers and the hot sun might have been getting to me.

But as I've always said: "Bad decisions make the best travel stories".

Whilst I wasn't 100% on the specifics of the design of my tattoo, I was certain that I knew who I wanted to do the work. I was training at Tiger Muay Thai in Chalong, Phuket and across the road, all of 15 metres away, was Chalong Ink. I'm pretty sure their location was planned based simply on the close proximity to drunk travelers at a Muay Thai training camp. Everyone was either trying to look tough or they were too drunk to properly make an informed decision. I wanted to look tough… just like my little sister.

I just read that out loud. Maybe I'll change my answer to "too drunk".

Note for my mother: I was not drunk. I'm simply trying to make my life sound more exciting than it actually is. Love you. I'll call you tonight.

I walked in to Chalong Ink and started to prepare myself by getting out of my pants. The guy working there was polite enough to stop me before I made myself too comfortable, and explained to me I needed to make an appointment to discuss what I would like done. I paid my 500 baht booking fee and agreed to return (fully pant-ed) at 6pm that night. Fast forward to 6pm and not surprisingly I was feeling a lot more comfortable about my decision, probably due to the amount of beer I had ingested while I was waiting.

Honestly, by that stage I was feeling a lot more comfortable about everything thanks to our good friends at that magical place, the Chang beer factory. I discussed with Bamboo Man (I'm not making this up, that was his name) what I would like done and where I would like it. He spoke no English so the "translator" (and I use that term in the loosest possible sense) tried to explain to the Thai local that the English speaking man in front of him wanted a saying tattooed on his chest in Latin.

Doesn't this sound like the start of a bad joke?

Bamboo Man asked to see what I had in mind. I took out my iPad to show him the design as I imagined it. In hindsight, that act probably increased the cost of my tattoo by about 3000 baht. Rookie mistake. Bamboo Man took my iPad and studied the design. He then nodded to a rough looking westerner and said, "Go".

I was a little confused as to where I was going, but I followed his friend outside and proceeded to make polite conversation whilst he shaved my chest with a pink razor. Suddenly I was wishing I had more beer. I headed back inside to Bamboo Man where he took out a pen and drew the words freehand across my chest. It seems this wasn't going to be a discussion about what I wanted, rather he was just going to go ahead and do the job and then charge me way more than he would have if I had come in with a design doodled on a dirty napkin. Oh well. As least they weren't going to charge me for the shaved chest.

After checking that he had spelled everything correctly, I checked that he had spelled everything correctly. Then, just to make sure, I checked that he had spelled everything correctly. No, I'm not in the process of having a stroke, I just want you to know how really paranoid I was about getting my first tattoo from someone who didn't speak English in a country where my (lack of) travel insurance wouldn't mean much if I ended up contracting AIDS. Plus, it would just look ridiculous.

I took my place on the cling wrap covered chair and watched Bamboo Man like a hawk while he collected needles, inks, gloves, alcohol rub, etc. It's strange but I actually felt like the whole operation was "above board" hygiene-wise. After being offered and rejecting the muscle relaxants on offer, I was ready for Bamboo Man to do his thing. HOLY SHIT THIS IS THE MOST PAIN I'VE BEEN IN SINCE…

I'm kidding. The chest surprisingly doesn't hurt all that much. It may have been the 642 Changs I had consumed that day. Or it may have been the muscle relaxants I decided to take after the first couple of minutes. Whatever it was, I felt great!

After three hours of being repeatedly stabbed with several needles attached to a stick, my tattoo was finally complete. I was eager to see it, and my shaved chest, in all its glory. I stood, and slowly turned to face the mirror. That's when I saw it… There, on my chest… Where I expected to see the words "de mortuis nil nisi bonum"… Was… Just that.

It seems Bamboo Man had in fact earned his title as 2nd best tattoo artist in all of Thailand for a very good reason. His artwork was faultless. His hygiene standards were impeccable. His muscle relaxants were free. AND I got a free chest shave. It seemed waiting a decade for my first tattoo was worth it.

But be warned; never show your tattoo artist you can afford an iPad.
