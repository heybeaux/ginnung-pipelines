# Starting to feel like family

After only 4 weeks at the academy, I feel like I've started to build relationships that will last a lifetime. I know being out of my comfort zone must add to my need to find people I can relate to. As a result I'm probably building these relationships up quickly, but they're awesome people so I don't mind.

Since I've been in China I've become good friends with a slightly unstable American, Will, and a hyperactive Colombian, Sebastian.

Will reminds me of my sister… Actually, that's a weird way to introduce him, but it's true. He has an evil sense of humour that no one appreciates. He teases people to the point that they want to cry, just to see if he can push them that far. He seems to be somewhat ADD… A conversation with him is like a roller-coaster ride with multiple paths it could take, but it's never the one you expect. He can be the biggest prick if he thinks you're an idiot, but if he likes you, you won't find a better mate. See? Just like my sister.;-)

Sebastian is like a little brother. He inspires me with his talents in the martial arts. He is one of the few people here at the academy who is genuinely passionate about what he does. And he is so good at it. Not just good… Amazing. Sensational. Watching him do his forms is actually art. His talent inspires me to train harder and perfect what I do. He told me today that after his time at the academy, he wants to join "Cirque du Soleil". It's such an amazing goal, I believe wholeheartedly that if that's what he has set his mind to, then that's what he'll achieve.

I really do look at these guys like family. I never thought I'd find friends this awesome here, but I'm glad I have. As difficult as training and the monotony of day-to-day life can be here, these guys make life a lot easier. I'm really grateful.
