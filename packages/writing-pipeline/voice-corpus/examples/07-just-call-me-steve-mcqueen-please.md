# Just call me Steve McQueen… Please?

I thought leaving Zhengzhou would be the last of my "dramas" in China. Unfortunately, some higher power was giggling his/her/its backside off after I allowed myself a sigh of relief. When we landed in Guangzhou I figured we'd sit in the plane for a few minutes while those passengers who were disembarking would go about their business, and then we'd be free to leave.

Almighty deity: Mr McQueen, are you serious? I delayed your flight for 9 hours. Do you seriously, SERIOUSLY think that was the only fun I had planned for you?

Me (Mr McQueen for the purpose of this dialogue, obviously): Um… Yes?

Almighty: Was that a question?

McQueen: No?

Almighty: …? Was that a question?

McQueen: Yes. I mean no. Wait… Am I getting out of China or not?

Pilot: Get off the plane. Take all your carry on luggage with you.

McQueen: … I guess not.

It seemed that I needed to go through the whole immigration/departing China paperwork, and obviously that couldn't have been done earlier. Fair enough, I'll give them that. How long could this possibly take?

Almighty: Bahahahahahahahah!

We were asked to break up into two groups. Those leaving us in Guangzhou, and those going on to Beijing. We were then asked to follow the airhostess down a corridor, up some stairs, down an escalator, and to the immigration/departure desks. The guy behind the desk asked me where my yellow card was. I explained to him that I hadn't been given one yet. He looked at me with absolute disgust and handed me a departure card. Of course I needed a pen too. That seemed to piss him off even more. I promptly completed my yellow card and handed it to him. I don't think he even looked at it. He was more interested in comparing my passport beard to my current beard. As if two people would have equally awesome beards! Mr Disgusted finally stamped my passport and waved me through. I couldn't help but say "Mate, you guys still shit in the street, don't give me those looks!" He must have thought I was saying goodbye. He still looked disgusted.

We then went up two escalators walked about 9 1/2km around 38 corners, climbed 4 ladders, went down 2 slides (one metal, one inflatable) and made our way through a house of mirrors to finally arrive at our boarding gate… Where we had to wait for about 30 minutes. "It's ok" I thought to myself, because I'm about to leave China. Right?

Almighty: Do you ACTUALLY want to know the answer?

When we were finally allowed to go through the boarding gate, I noticed that some of the posters at one of the turns looked familiar. Understandable, since I'm sure they have thousands of the same posters all over the airport. However it soon dawned on me that I had just completed a half ironman race around Guangzhou airport, just to arrive back in exactly the same place.

McQueen: Almighty?

Almighty: Yes?

McQueen: I'm going to become really good at Muay Thai in Thailand and kick your arse.

Almighty: I'm going to crash the plane.

McQueen: … Ok, I take that back.

Almighty: Thought you might.

Long story short(er than I could easily make it), there was no delay once we boarded the plane and I made it to Bangkok bright and early Monday morning. I finally made it to Thailand. A new set of adventures can begin.

Almighty: You have NO idea… Hahahahahaha.
