# DOMS now means Days Of Muscle Soreness

I thought I'd be sensible and have a week off of training after pushing myself training Kung Fu in China for 5 months. Turns out there's this voice in my head (as well as a talking monkey named Jeeves who rides a unicycle and brings me Merlot) that won't let me sit down for more then 5 minutes. Seriously, I've walked away from my laptop three times just writing these three sentences! Anyway, ADD aside, after my first day of settling in to camp life and buying every single singlet and pair of shorts I could find, I got bored! I seriously thought I could chill out for a full week. I thought I could drink beer and play drinking games. I thought I could lounge by the pool. I thought I could by more singlets and shorts because this place is incredibly hot and humid. Like, for real! My jocks only last about 20 minutes before I feel like I need to change them. You're thinking about my wet underwear now, aren't you? I love writing.

I started by easing into training. I checked the timetable for Tuesday and saw there was a Body Fit class in the afternoon. Easy. Those classes are for girls. A bit of cardio, a bit of bodyweight stuff and I'd feel a lot better about drinking beer. Jeeves tried to pass me a Merlot, but I turned it down, grabbed a bottle of water and headed to class. There are no words to describe how out of my depths I felt. We had a visiting trainer from Hawaii, Scott, who was incredibly fit and incredibly kind. Noticing I was new, he came over and introduced himself and explained what he had in store for the class. 3 minute rounds, 30 second break, 17 times through. Hold on… That's 51 minutes of actual working time! That can't be right? Was it too late for that Merlot? Jeeves? Anyone?

My coping mechanism when I'm uncomfortable or being pushed to my limits is to smile and laugh. It usually inspires other people around me, when they see me struggling, but still being incredible positive about the whole situation. It also allows me to convince myself that it can't be that bad if I'm still having such a good time. Weird logic, but to date it has always worked for me. By the end of that session I was giggling like a little girl. My legs were killing me and I couldn't push out a single push up even if my life depended on it, but I had finished. Time for that beer.

The rest of my week was very much the same. I was heading to classes I didn't particularly want to do simply because I had so much energy I couldn't sit around. By the end of the week I had completed sessions in Body Fit, Crossfit, cross training, yoga and beach bootcamp, as well my own weights sessions in the gym and a couple of spin sessions on the bikes. When I laughed, my stomach muscles hurt so much I cried. When I had a shower, I needed to take a seat with me because standing was too much effort. When I dropped something, it stayed there. I did however finish the week feeling a sense of accomplishment. Even though I could have taken a week off the voice in my head didn't let me. It was more comfortable putting in the effort and training. I left Australia to discover the world and find out more about myself. I want to better myself. I want to improve. I don't want to return to Australia the same person I was when I left. I really am starting to see changes in myself and I love it.

Now, where's Jeeves with that Merlot?
