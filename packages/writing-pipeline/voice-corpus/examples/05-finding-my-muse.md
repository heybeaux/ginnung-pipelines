# Finding my Muse

Writing is incredibly fun. Storytelling is even better. Personally, I'm not much of a writer. Honestly, I can't describe an apple in such a way that you know the feeling of its skin against yours and the explosion of flavour as you bite into it. To be honest, I'd rather eat the apple than write about it. I'm a storyteller. I like to have people hanging on my every word as I take them on a journey. I love the reactions. I love hearing people argue after hearing a story, like I must be lying because "stuff like that doesn't actually happen". Writing doesn't evoke reactions like that.

Storytelling is an art. And all arts need a Muse. Now, I don't have some Greek Goddess inspiring my writing, at least, not that I'm aware of. I've met some amazing people, seen some pretty messed up things, and collected more than enough stories to be the life of almost any party I attend. You would think these things alone would be enough to inspire. Sadly, they are not. Most of the time, I do something new or different and think "that's pretty cool" and that's where it ends. I have no desire to write about it. It's just not interesting enough. And that is where I get caught up in writing. I'm better than that! I'm a storyteller! Even the everyday can be made interesting with a little passion and the right words. A Muse inspires this passion.

I apologise to the people who have been following my blog. I apologise for showing a lot of photos and not telling the story that goes with it. A few lines saying "isn't this pretty?" is what you'd expect from a "Professional Travel Writer". Again, I'm a storyteller. From now on when I write, I will do so with such a passion and fire that you won't be able to help but devour every word and syllable. I will make you laugh. I will make you cry. I will work my arse off to make you feel something. Anything!! I will be the one thing that holds your attention for more than a minute. My stories may last a minute or a lifetime, but they will be the single most interesting thing you ever experience. My stories will inspire you all.

I'm a storyteller. It's my gift.
