# My last day as a Kung Fu student (in China)

Friday. The end of the week is finally here. With it comes an ending. An ending of a journey I've been on for 5 months now. I came to China to be challenged. To study a style of Kung Fu I've been interested in for over 10 years. To learn about other cultures and meet people I never would have had the opportunity to meet had I stayed at home. I'm not totally sure how I feel about this whole "moving on" thing. A part of me is obviously sad about leaving behind some people I've grown very close to. Another part of me is understandably very excited for the new challenges that await me in Thailand. The thing I'm trying to work out though is which feeling or emotion had squeezed its way into the little.647882% left over? Is it happiness? Am I glad I'm leaving? Is it fear? Am I going to get arrested and end up in a Thai jail? Actually… That does sound a tiny bit fun… Either way, it's the not knowing of this tiny little sliver of emotion that leaves me feeling a little off about what's to come next.

All this emotional ramble aside… I'm really looking forward to traveling again. Being in transit can be dead shit boring. But with it comes a kind of buzz. What's next? Where do I take my bag? Where is my ticket? Where's my wallet? Fuck. Where's my wallet? Holy shit!! I've lost my effing wa… Oh wait, I'm holding it. Never mind. And when the transit side of things is over, you're in a band new place. New people. New experiences. New memories to be made. A new home to be made.

There's just something about the drug of traveling that makes it more addictive than anything else.
