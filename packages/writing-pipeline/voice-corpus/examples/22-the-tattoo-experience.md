# The tattoo experience

I don't know why we thought it was a good idea to head out in search of a tattoo place armed with nothing but a business card (in Chinese of course), but the adventurous spirit in us took over and we were powerless. We asked the nearest Chinese man where we could find the place. He simply pointed. No words, no other gestures. Great start. After walking for a total of 30 seconds we came across a small place in an alley with dragons and skulls painted on the walls. It was probably a little presumptuous, but we figured we had found it. There was a hilarious exchange between Will and the tattoo artist via some translator app on the computer. The conversation went a little like…
Artist: "What you do?"
Will: "Can I get a tattoo here?"
Artist: "Yes, but not for free."
Will: "Are the needles clean?"
Artist: "It is once off. How big do you want it?"
Will: "How much?"
Artist: "¥300." - About AU$50.
This exchange was only interrupted twice by the artist's girlfriend who wanted to play solitaire or some other card game on the computer between responses. Classic.

All in all it was a rather painless experience (for me). Will is happy with the overall result, and can now cross something off of his bucket list. Photos to follow.
