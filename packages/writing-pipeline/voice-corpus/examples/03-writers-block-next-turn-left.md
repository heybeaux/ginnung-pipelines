# Writer's block - next turn left

There is a curse that many writers know all too well. The curse of writer's block. For travel writers and storytellers it is particularly hard since we experience so much adventure, but are sometimes unsure of how to fully explain the experiences to our families, friends and readers. I lay awake in bed the other night, my mind racing with a thousand thoughts and stories, a book idea, a movie, my next adventure, a poem, a Facebook update (yes, that is a legitimate form of writing), and at the same time I felt I had nothing of value. The thought crossed my mind "Is this writer's block?" As I let my mind wander, I started to imagine that writer's block wasn't a curse, rather a place. Imagine if writers could pack up their laptops and head to "Writer's Block", the indie/hipster place hidden away down some dark alley, surrounded by a unique coffee shop or two, an old, dusty book shop and a pub that is rarely frequented. What if writers could come together in this place and inspire one another? What if I could sit down with other travel writers and storytellers, be they friends or complete strangers, and we could tell our stories? We could bounce our thoughts and passions off of one another while a scribe sat in the corner of the room, hastily jotting down notes for us. At the end of our stay, on our way to the door, the young scribe would hand us his notes with a simple and polite "Thank you for coming to Writer's Block". I could leave this place with my stories in hand, a traveller with a tale to tell once again.

We travellers pride ourselves on not being like tourists. We eat where we shouldn't. We get drunk with complete strangers. We hire scooters when our insurance doesn't cover us. We look for that situation that screams "not really the best idea, what would your mother say?" and we embrace it wholeheartedly. A tourist tells you about how expensive the cab ride was. A traveller tells you how they had a really interesting 3 hour bus ride talking to complete strangers and then ended up somewhere completely different to where they thought they were going. These stories swim around in a travel writers mind all the time. Sometimes we get lucky and can put one into words. Other times they remain stories to be told over a few beers because we could never quite gather the right words to explain it in writing.

If only I could catch the next bus to "Writers Block".

In fact, maybe that's the very bus I'm sitting on right now…
