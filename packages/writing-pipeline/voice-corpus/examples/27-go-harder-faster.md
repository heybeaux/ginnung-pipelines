# Go. Harder. Faster.

Yesterday I was introduced to perhaps one of the most important aspects of martial arts… Conditioning. Our class went to the small training room when we were shown how to stand quietly and meditate in order to build our Chi. After 30 minutes of manipulating this internal energy and "washing my face" with it, we were lead outside to begin conditioning. I was feeling rather relaxed from the meditating session so wasn't really expecting anything too strenuous. I couldn't have been more wrong. I was allocated a tree with another student and told to hit the tree 200 times with each hand in a chopping motion. The first 30-40 hurt a lot. It was about -5 outside and the tree wasn't exactly smooth. I felt like I had worked through the hard part when my Sifu came up to show my how I should be hitting it to get the most out of the session. He doesn't speak much English, but after showing me how to condition correctly, he said the all-to-familiar words: Go. Harder. Faster. By the end of the first 200 I was in agony, and was still yet to start on the other hand. By the time I had completed the task I thought I was home free. My frozen finger tips would have agreed. Unfortunately the chops were followed by punching, back hands, palm strikes, fingers in a claw type position, knuckles, elbows, shoulders, knees, shins, headbutting and finally a stress position that involved resting my forehead against the tree and walking my feet out far enough that I had all my weight on my neck. I was to hold that for as long as possible. It's amazing how relaxing that felt after such an "easy" session.
